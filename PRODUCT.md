# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences arrive at the same site with the same question — "can this person build the thing I need, and build it well?"

- **Local Mérida businesses** (shop owners, small/medium brands): often non-technical, evaluating whether to commission a Shopify store or a custom web build. They judge competence by how the site itself looks and feels.
- **Agencies and remote clients**: studios and teams who can read craft fluently and want proof of a senior hand before booking a project.

The job to be done for both: quickly form a high trust verdict on Ivan's skill, then start a conversation (primarily WhatsApp / the contact flow). The site is in Spanish today, serving a Mérida-first but remote-capable practice.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio and freelance landing for Ivan Garcia, web developer in Mérida. It exists to convert a cold visitor into a qualified lead by demonstrating craft rather than claiming it: the work showcase, the services, and the site's own execution are the argument. Success = a serious prospect (local or remote) reaches the contact step convinced Ivan is the right, high-quality choice.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, modern. The voice of a senior engineer who sweats the details and lets the work speak. Self-assured without bravado; technical without being cold. Emotional goal in the first five seconds: *"this person is a serious craftsman."* Copy is direct and specific, never salesy or buzzword-driven.

## Anti-references

- **The generic dev-portfolio template.** No cookie-cutter "I'm a developer" layout: no identical project cards in a 3-up grid, no skill-bar percentages, no stock hero with a centered avatar and a wave emoji, no "Tech Stack" icon wall as the personality.
- Sterile corporate / SaaS-agency landing pages (faceless blue-gradient hero, feature-grid sameness). This is a person, not a company.
- Motion-as-showreel: every effect firing at once. Animation stays intentional and restrained.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the portfolio.** Execution quality is the primary proof of skill — every detail is a hiring signal, so nothing ships rough.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Demonstrate competence through the work and the craft, not adjectives about it.
3. **Confidence through restraint.** Precise typography, deliberate spacing, and one decisive accent beat visual noise. Motion serves meaning.
4. **Earn the contact.** Every section moves a skeptical visitor one step closer to reaching out; the contact path is always within reach.
5. **One signature, held consistently.** The dark Framer canvas, blue accent, and gradient spotlight cards are the identity — extend them, don't dilute them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against the dark canvas (watch the muted-gray ramp — `--ink-muted` on `--canvas` needs verification), large/display text ≥3:1. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states. `prefers-reduced-motion` is honored across all animations (already established in `global.css`). Semantic HTML and meaningful link/button labels. Structure should anticipate an eventual Spanish/English split even though the site is Spanish-only today.
